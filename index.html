I am a guest lecturer in the Department of Physics at MAMO College, Manassery.

I was a Post doctoral fellow at the Raman Research Institute, where my research was on nonequilibrium statistical physics, with a focus on heat transport mentored by Prof. Dibyendu Roy in the Theoretical Physics Department.

I completed my PhD at the Chennai Mathematical Institute (CMI), where my research, supervised by Prof. Govind S. Krishnaswami, focused on classical and quantum integrable systems and field theories and related topics in theoretical physics.

My doctoral thesis addresses the dynamics and integrability of a scalar field theory dual to the 1+1 dimensional SU(2) Principal chiral model and its reduction to a novel mechanical system describing a class of nonlinear screw-type waves.

My primary interests are in integrable systems and open quantum systems, though I am open to exploring problems in other areas of theoretical and mathematical physics.
